Spatial Analysis of Potential Planting Areas

In order to achieve the goal of overal tree canopy cover of 35% by 2051, it is of utmost importance to understand and visualize the potentially suitable tree planting areas to make informed decision about tree planting. The goal of the team is to accurately identify potential planting areas in the City of Peterborough with detailed delineation of geographic attributes.

A series of spatial analyses were performed to generate layers of potential planting areas.

GIS Software

ArcGIS Pro is the primary GIS software used.

Data Used in Spatial Analysis

Three major types of spatial data were used in the spatial anaylsis of Potential Planting Areas:

  • Vector Data
  • LiDAR Data
  • Georeferenced Aerial Imagery

Selected above-ground and underground utilities, infrastructure and physical structures, which prohibit tree planting in a location, were identified and defined as conflicts. Some examples are overhead powerlines, roads, existing trees, streetlights, hydrants, sanitary systems, storm management systems, etc.

Spatial Anaylsis Workflow

The major analysis phases are as follows:

  1. Reprojecting data
  2. Clipping to study area extent
  3. Extract a suitable base layer for further analysis
  4. Creating buffered areas for selected above-ground and underground conflicts
  5. Erasing conflicts from the base layer
  6. Classifying land ownership
  7. Delineating areas suitable for different sizes of trees
  8. Enriching layers with salt spray tolerance and zoning informaiton
  9. Analyzeing statistical results

ArcGIS Pro Toolbox & Models

Alongside the spatial analysis, a series of models were created within a custom toolbox in ArcGIS Pro to record the complete workflow and geoprocessing tools used for data preprocessing, data processing, and spatial analysis. The toolbox and models allow the client to rerun the PPA analysis with new datasets in the future as needed.

Spatial Anaylsis Results

Features within the resulting potential planting area layers have a wide range of attributes about land ownership, suitablility for tree sizes, salt spray tolerance, and zoning information.

What's Next?

The resulting potential planting area layers would serve as an updated baseline study on the extent of available planting sites within the city boundaries. They would also be the stepping stone for the client to develop tree planting programs.

In addition, the potential planting area layers were combined with a tree species database in an interactive web mapping application. That application suggest the most suitable tree species to plant when a potential planting area is selected. Check it out here!
