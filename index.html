Optimizing Tree Planting for the Green Future of Peterborough

Project Abstract

Objectives

    Identify suitable tree planting areas by an updated potential planting area (PPA) layer with detailed delineation from physical structures that may restrict tree growth.

    Recommend suitable tree species to plant in a location within the PPAs based on the adjacent environment and tree characteristics by a web mapping application.

    Visualize the tree inventory for the public through a web map.

Client

Our Client is the City of Peterborough.

Throughout the project, we have received invaluable support from the City's Urban Forest Program Manager and GIS Technologist.

Background

In the view of the significance of the ecological benefits provided by urban trees on its public and private lands, the City aimed to achieve a goal of overal tree canopy coverage of 35% by 2051. This goal requires a reliable workflow to identify potential planting areas and to determine what trees to plant. Public participation is also crucial considering the significance of tree planting over private properties.
