Optimizing Tree Planting for the Green Future of Peterborough

Project Abstract

Recognizing the significance of trees in harvesting numerous ecological benefits and combating climate change, the City of Peterborough has set a target to expand the overall tree canopy coverage to 35% by 2051. To this end, it is crucial to identify suitable areas in the city for tree planting, known as potential planting areas. This insight supports the decision-making process in selecting tree species to plant in specific locations and facilitates knowledge transfer especially to the wider public. As a result, the primary objective of this project is to generate high-quality potential planting area data; the secondary objectives are to create a tree species recommendation tool for office staff use and a web map of tree inventory for public use.

In support of the City's canopy goal and urban forest management strategies, the CENGIS Team proposed three deliverables: (1) Classifying and analyzing the potential planting areas in the City using multiple data sources, including vector data, LiDAR Data, and aerial imagery, through a series of spatial analyses; (2) Creating a web mapping application which recommends tree species based on specific potential planting area location in terms of proximity to various obstacles and geographic characteristics; and (3) Creating a user-friendly web map of tree inventory and tree zone summary statistics for the general public.

Objectives

    Identify suitable tree planting areas by an updated potential planting area layer with detailed delineation from physical structures that may restrict tree growth.

    Recommend suitable tree species to plant in a location within the potential planting areas based on the adjacent environment and tree characteristics by a web mapping application.

    Visualize the tree inventory for the public through a web map.

Client

Our Client is the City of Peterborough.

Throughout the project, we have received invaluable support from the City's Urban Forest Program Manager and GIS Technologist.

Background

In the view of the significance of the ecological benefits provided by urban trees on its public and private lands, the City aimed to achieve a goal of overal tree canopy coverage of 35% by 2051. This goal requires a reliable workflow to identify potential planting areas and to determine what trees to plant. Public participation is also crucial considering the significance of tree planting over privately owned lands.
